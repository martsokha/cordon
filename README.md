# Cordon

**Genre:** Management / Trading Sim / Narrative  
**Tone:** Gritty, tense, darkly humorous — post-apocalyptic exclusion zone meets Papers, Please  
**Perspective:** First-person, fixed location (your bunker/stall in the Zone)  
**Platform:** Web (initially)

## Premise

You are a trader operating out of a bunker deep in the Zone — a quarantined, anomaly-riddled wasteland. Scavengers come to you to buy, sell, and barter. You never leave — the Zone comes to you.

Your goal: survive, profit, and navigate the politics of the Zone's factions. Every deal you make ripples outward. Sell cheap meds to a wounded drifter? He might come back with rare relics. Gouge a soldier from the Order? His squad might "inspect" your bunker tomorrow.

## Core Fantasy

You are not the hero — you're the guy everyone needs. You sit behind your counter, size up whoever walks through the door, and decide what they're worth to you. The tension isn't in gunfights; it's in the deal. Do you trust this guy? Can you afford to say no? What happens when two factions both want the same thing?

## Inspirations

- **Papers, Please**: moral weight in mundane transactions, atmosphere through repetition
- **S.T.A.L.K.E.R. series**: setting, tone, factions, lore
- **Darkest Dungeon**: risk management, sending others into danger
